His edge is blending **reactive** (key-level-based) and **predictive** (direction-based) approaches, with heavy emphasis on the former. He teaches ICT/SMC concepts but is critical of traders who obsess over daily bias at the expense of key levels. His trademark is catching 5:1 to 15:1 reward-to-risk trades by waiting for price to reach HTF key levels and entering on LTF confirmations.

## The Setup(s)

### Setup 1: HTF Key Level + LTF Confirmation (Universal)
- **Trigger:** Price reaches a pre-identified HTF key level (imbalance/FVG, order block, S&R, supply/demand)
- **Timeframes:** HTF = 4H (or weekly/daily), LTF = 15min (or 1H/5min). Relative alignment: weekly→4H, daily→1H, 4H→15min, 15min→1min
- **Entry:** On LTF after confirmation at the key level — rejection wick, sweep/fakeout, or acceptance (close outside the level). Entry at the retest of the rectangle or immediately on closure outside.
- **Stop:** Above/below the invalidation point of the HP range (swing high/low or entry structure)
- **Target:** Next HTF key level in the direction of the move. Trail stops at minor levels; take partials.
- **Does NOT apply when:** No clear HTF key level present; price in middle of range (bad price position); level not anchored by prior respect; no displacement from the level

### Setup 2: 1-Minute Scalping — Rectangle Method (Specific)
- **Trigger:** 15min pattern of Weakness (failure to close above a high or below a low at origin) + Strength (break of structure) + Imbalance created within the range
- **Timeframes:** 15min for structure/pattern, 1min for entry
- **Entry:** 
  1. Mark 15min high/low inside or near an unfilled imbalance that's part of the pattern
  2. Wait for a 15min candle to take that high/low AND close back (weak rejection — candle closes below the high for shorts, above the low for longs)
  3. Draw rectangle from candle close to the wick
  4. Switch to 1min; enter when 1min candle CLOSES outside the rectangle
  5. Optional: place limit order at the rectangle edge for sniper entry on retest
- **Stop:** Above/below the rectangle (or above the new wick if a higher wick formed before the close-out)
- **Target:** Next market structure level (break of structure target), or fixed 3:1 minimum. Can extend to 5:1–13:1 if structure supports it.
- **Does NOT apply when:** No weakness+strength pattern; no imbalance near the high/low; 15min candle body closes through the imbalance (disrespect); outside kill zone sessions

### Setup 3: 15-Minute Gold — FVG Continuation (Specific)
- **Trigger:** High probability range with all three: break of structure + imbalance (FVG) + strong invalidation point. Range must be **fresh** (no direction change since the BOS).
- **Timeframes:** 15min only (standalone, though HTF context improves it)
- **Entry:** 
  1. Identify HP range — anchored, displaced (BOS 1.5x-2x last leg), range filled (retraced to OTE)
  2. Wait for price to retrace into the FVG WITH RESPECT (no candle body closes outside the FVG)
  3. Enter when a candle CLOSES outside the FVG in the direction of the HP range
  4. Extra: 50% of the FVG is the highest-probability touch point; immediate rejection (liquidity sweep) = no-drawdown entry
- **Stop:** Above/below the confirmation candle or the FVG extreme
- **Target:** Fixed 2:1 minimum; can extend to next structure level
- **Does NOT apply when:** Range is unfilled (no significant retracement); no real displacement (BOS < 1.5x last leg); old/stale range (direction already changed); outside high-volume sessions

### Setup 4: CCT Continuation/Reversal (Universal Framework)
- **Trigger:** Previous candle (any TF) closes; next candle opens
- **Timeframes:** Any — weekly, daily, 4H, 15min. Higher TF candle = bias, lower TF = entry
- **Continuation entry:** Candle opens AGAINST the direction first (below open for bullish, above open for bearish), then breaks the previous candle's high/low. Wait for displacement beyond previous high/low.
- **Reversal entry:** Candle opens WITH the direction first (above open for bullish, below open for bearish), FAILS to displace beyond previous high/low, reverses. Occurs at HTF key levels.
- **Stop:** Beyond the pivot (swing high/low of the entry structure)
- **Target:** Next HTF key level or previous candle's opposite extreme
- **Does NOT apply when:** No clear HTF key level for reversals; indecision (price oscillates around open without breaking either extreme)

## Key Concepts

### High Probability Range (3 Criteria)
A range (swing low to swing high) qualifies as high probability when:
1. **Anchored** — the base (start) of the range is held by another key level or structure. It reacted off something (imbalance, order block, prior S&R). Not "floating in the middle of nowhere."
2. **Displacement** — a strong break of structure at the end of the range. The BOS move must be **1.5x to 2x** the size of the last leg within the range. A single-candle close beyond a swing point is NOT a real displacement.
3. **Range Fill** — price retraces significantly into the range, at minimum to 50%, ideally to the OTE zone (61.8%–79% fib).

### Price Position
Where price sits relative to the current range. Ask: "Am I chasing?" and "Where would my stop loss go?"
- **Good:** At discount (below 50%) for buys; at premium (above 50%, ideally 61.8%–79%) for sells. Stop loss is tight and logical.
- **Bad:** Chasing breakouts, middle of ranges, after expansion without retracement. Stop loss is too wide relative to target.

### CCT (Candle Continuity Theory)
Trading the next candle based on the previous one. Focus on open, high (bullish), low (bearish).
- **Continuation:** Move opposite direction first → holds the extreme → breaks previous extreme. Higher probability, works with trend.
- **Reversal:** Move same direction first → fails to displace → reverses. Only valid at HTF key levels.
- **Indecision:** Price oscillates around open, breaks neither extreme. Stay out.

### Displacement Rule
A valid break of structure requires the displacing move to be **1.5x–2x** the size of the last leg being broken. Measure pips from swing to swing. This filters out fakeouts and single-candle traps.

### Market Structure Shift Trap
The common "break of last low/high = reversal" pattern is frequently a **trap** (continuation, not reversal). Valid MSS requires:
- Alignment with a HTF key level (never in the "middle of nowhere")
- Break of **two** significant lows/highs (not just the last one)
- "Edge School Method": break below a low that previously respected a broken high

### Key Level Types (Interchangeable)
- Imbalances / Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) — 3-candle pattern where wicks don't overlap
- Order blocks — the last candle before a BOS
- Supply & demand zones
- Support & resistance (S&R)
- Engulfing candles
- "The Edge Model key levels" — taught in his paid community, not fully disclosed

### FVG Respect vs Disrespect
- **Respect:** Candle bodies do NOT close outside the FVG. Wick can penetrate. This keeps the FVG "alive."
- **Disrespect:** A candle body closes through/outside the FVG. The level is nullified.
- **50% of FVG:** The most significant level within a wide FVG. Touches at 50% give best sniper entries.

### Strength vs Weakness
- **Strength:** Displacing/disrespecting a key level — closing beyond a swing point. Signals continuation.
- **Weakness:** Failing to close beyond a key level — wick through but body closes back. Signals potential reversal. Weakness at the origin of a move + strength at the destination = highest-probability pattern.

### Fresh vs Old Structure
- **Fresh:** Most recent break of structure; price hasn't changed direction since. Trade ONLY fresh ranges.
- **Old:** Prior structure before a direction change. Ignore for entries.

### Price Behavior at Key Levels
- **Approach:** Impulsive (aggressive, no pullback → exhaustion → sharp reversal) OR Corrective (channel/flag, clean retracement → continuation). Avoid ranging/slow approaches.
- **Inside (triggers):** Rejection (long wick), Sweep/Fakeout (take a level beyond and reverse), Acceptance (close outside the level with strength).
- **Rule:** Match strong approach with weakness inside, OR weak approach with strength inside.

### Kill Zones / High-Volume Sessions (EST / New York time)
- Asia: 20:00–00:00 (8 PM–12 AM)
- London: 02:00–05:00 (2 AM–5 AM)
- New York: 07:00–10:00 (7 AM–10 AM)
Only trade during these windows for higher probability.

## Gates (Draft Pipeline)

Gate 1: Kill Zone Check — is current time within a high-volume session?
  Pass: Current time is within Asia (20:00-00:00), London (02:00-05:00), or NY (07:00-10:00) EST
  Fail: Outside all kill zones → SKIP

Gate 2: HTF Key Level Present — does the HTF (4H for 15min entry, daily for 1H entry) have an unfilled imbalance/FVG or other key level within recent range?
  Pass: At least one HTF key level (FVG, order block, S&R) exists within the last 50-100 HTF candles
  Fail: No identifiable HTF key level → SKIP

Gate 3: Price Position — is price at a good position relative to the recent HP range?
  Pass: Price is at/above 61.8% fib of the bearish HP range (for shorts) OR at/below 38.2% fib of the bullish HP range (for longs)
  Fail: Price in middle of range or chasing a breakout → SKIP

Gate 4: High Probability Range Identified — does the recent range satisfy all 3 HP criteria?
  Pass: Range is anchored (reacted off a level), displaced (BOS ≥ 1.5x last leg), and filled (retraced ≥ 50%)
  Fail: Range missing any of the 3 criteria → SKIP

Gate 5: Direction Alignment (CCT / HTF Candle Bias) — does the LTF setup align with HTF candle bias?
  Pass: 15min setup direction matches the current 4H candle bias (continuation) OR setup is a reversal at a clear 4H key level
  Fail: Setup direction opposes HTF candle bias with no HTF key level to anchor it → SKIP

Gate 6: Weakness + Strength Pattern — does the setup show weakness at origin AND strength at destination?
  Pass: Failure to close beyond a swing point at the origin (weakness) AND a valid BOS (≥ 1.5x) at the destination (strength)
  Fail: Missing weakness or strength → SKIP

Gate 7: FVG Respect at Entry Zone — is there an unfilled FVG at the entry area showing respect?
  Pass: An FVG exists near the entry; candle bodies have not closed outside it; a candle has taken the FVG extreme and closed back (weak rejection)
  Fail: FVG fully filled/disrespected, or no FVG near entry → SKIP

Gate 8: Entry Trigger (Rectangle / Close-Out) — has the 1min/15min entry trigger fired?
  Pass: Trigger candle took the FVG extreme and closed back; rectangle drawn; 1min (or entry TF) candle closed outside the rectangle in setup direction
  Fail: No close-out yet → WAIT
  Fail: Close-out happened but in wrong direction → SKIP

Gate 9: Stop & Target Defined — can we set a logical stop and target?
  Pass: Stop can be placed above/below rectangle or swing point with RR ≥ 2:1; target is next HTF key level or structural level
  Fail: Stop too wide (RR < 2:1) or no clear target → SKIP

Gate 10: Final — set levels, mark TAKE
  Pass: All above passed
  Action: Set result.direction ("long"/"short"), entry (close-out price or rectangle edge), stop (above/below rectangle or swing), target (next HTF key level or fixed RR level), status = TAKE

## Anti-Patterns

- **Chasing breakouts** — "entering immediately after the breakout is a bad price position." Wait for retracement.
- **Trading the middle of a range** — "you cannot buy here. You sell here." Only extremes.
- **Obsessing over daily bias** — "a lot of ICT traders blame wrong daily bias... The real problem is poor key level identification."
- **Single-candle BOS** — "closing one candle below a low... is not a real displacement." Need 1.5x-2x size.
- **Market structure shift in isolation** — "it's a trap... better to look for longs instead of shorts." Need HTF alignment.
- **Trading unfilled ranges** — "at some point price will reverse and fill the range." Avoid ranges with no significant retracement.
- **Entering after expansion without retracement** — "after expansion moves, a lot of time we have retracement." Wait for pullback.
- **Trading old structure** — "the moment we have a change of direction... I'm not trading anywhere from here." Only fresh ranges.
- **Reversal without HTF key level** — "only valid at higher time frame key levels."
- **Forcing entries when HTF candle bias opposes** — "if you had a short setup and a long setup, take the one aligned with the higher time frame candle."
